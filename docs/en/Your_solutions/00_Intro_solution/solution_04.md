To rearrange the formula $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$ for $g$, we need to isolate $g$ one step at a time.

### 1. The Original Formula

The period ($T$) of a simple pendulum depends on its length ($L$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$):


$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

### 2. Isolate the Square Root

Divide both sides of the equation by $2\pi$ to get the square root term by itself:


$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

### 3. Remove the Square Root

To get rid of the square root, square both sides of the equation:


$$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \left(\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}\right)^2$$

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

### 4. Move $g$ to the Numerator

Since $g$ is currently in the denominator on the right side, multiply both sides by $g$ to move it to the other side:


$$g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L$$

### 5. Final Step: Isolate $g$

To get $g$ alone, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of the fraction $\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}$, which is $\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$:


$$g = L \cdot \frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$$

### Final Rearranged Formula:

$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$
