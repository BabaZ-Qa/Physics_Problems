To calculate the components of a vector, we use trigonometric functions to resolve the vector's magnitude into its horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) parts.

### 1. Identify the Given Values

* **Magnitude of Vector $\vec{A}$ ($A$):** 15
* **Angle ($\theta$):** 60°

---

### 2. Calculate the Horizontal Component ($A_x$)

The horizontal component is the "adjacent" side of the triangle formed by the vector. We use the **cosine** function:


$$A_x = A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$

Substitute the values:


$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$

Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:


$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5$$


**$A_x = 7.5$**

---

### 3. Calculate the Vertical Component ($A_y$)

The vertical component is the "opposite" side of the triangle. We use the **sine** function:


$$A_y = A \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Substitute the values:


$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$

Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:


$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866$$


**$A_y \approx 12.99$**

---

### Final Result:

The components of the vector $\vec{A}$ are:

* **Horizontal ($A_x$):** 7.5
* **Vertical ($A_y$):** 12.99 (or $7.5\sqrt{3}$)
