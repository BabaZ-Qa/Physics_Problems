This is a classic puzzle that sounds complicated if you try to track each individual trip the fly makes. However, there is a much simpler way to solve it by focusing on **time** rather than the fly's specific path.

### Step 1: Determine how long the bicycle travels

Since the fly is "crushed" when the bicycle hits the wall, the fly's travel time is exactly the same as the bicycle's travel time.

* **Distance to wall ($d$):** 10 meters
* **Bicycle speed ($v_b$):** 1 m/s

Using the formula $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$:


$$t = \frac{10}{1}$$


**$t = 10$ seconds**

---

### Step 2: Determine the fly's total travel time

The fly starts moving at the exact same moment as the bicycle and stops moving only when the bicycle hits the wall. Therefore, the fly is in the air for the entire **10 seconds**.

---

### Step 3: Calculate the total distance the fly travels

Regardless of how many times the fly turns around or how complex its path is, it maintains a constant speed throughout the entire duration.

* **Fly's speed ($v_f$):** 2 m/s
* **Total time ($t$):** 10 seconds

Using the formula $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$:


$$\text{Distance} = 2 \times 10$$


**$\text{Distance} = 20$ meters**

---

### Final Result:

The fly travels a total of **20 meters** before the bicycle reaches the wall.
