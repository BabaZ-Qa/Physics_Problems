To find the spring constant $k$ and the total mechanical energy $E$, we will compare the given displacement equation to the standard formula for simple harmonic motion (SHM).

### 1. Identify the Given Values
From the problem, we have:
* Mass $m = 10 \text{ kg}$
* Displacement equation: $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$

The standard form for SHM is:
$$x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)$$

By matching the terms, we find:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
* **Angular frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$

---

### 2. Solve for the Spring Constant ($k$)
The relationship between angular frequency, mass, and the spring constant is given by the formula:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

To solve for $k$, we rearrange the formula:
$$k = m \cdot \omega^2$$

Substitute the known values:
$$k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$$

Using the approximation $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$:
**$k \approx 9870 \text{ N/m}$**

---

### 3. Solve for Total Mechanical Energy ($E$)
The total mechanical energy in a mass-spring system is constant and is equal to the maximum potential energy at the point of maximum displacement (the amplitude):
$$E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$

Substitute the values for $k$ and $A$:
$$E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) (0.2)^2$$
$$E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) (0.04)$$
$$E = 500\pi^2 \cdot 0.04$$
$$E = 20\pi^2 \text{ J}$$

Using the approximation $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$:
**$E \approx 197.4 \text{ J}$**
