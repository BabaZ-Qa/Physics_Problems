To solve for the speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing, we use the **Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. This states that the potential energy ($PE$) at the release point is converted into kinetic energy ($KE$) at the lowest point.

### 1. Identify the Geometry
To find the potential energy, we first need the vertical height ($h$) the bob rises when it is pulled back to $15^\circ$.



* **Length of the string ($L$):** $1.0 \text{ m}$
* **Release angle ($\theta$):** $15^\circ$
* **Height at the bottom:** $0$ (our reference point)
* **Height at the release point ($h$):** Calculated using the formula $h = L - L\cos\theta$ or:
$$h = L(1 - \cos\theta)$$

Substitute the values:
$$h = 1.0(1 - \cos(15^\circ))$$
$$h \approx 1.0(1 - 0.9659)$$
$$h \approx 0.0341 \text{ m}$$

---

### 2. Apply Conservation of Energy
The energy at the top (all potential) must equal the energy at the bottom (all kinetic):
$$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Since the mass ($m$) appears on both sides, it cancels out. This means the speed of the pendulum does **not** depend on how heavy the bob is.

---

### 3. Solve for Velocity ($v$)
Rearranging the equation to solve for $v$:
$$v^2 = 2gh$$
$$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Using $g \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$ and our calculated $h$:
$$v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 0.0341}$$
$$v = \sqrt{0.669}$$
**$v \approx 0.818 \text{ m/s}$**

---

### Summary of Results
* **Vertical Height ($h$):** $\approx 0.034 \text{ m}$
* **Speed at Bottom ($v$):** $\approx 0.82 \text{ m/s}$

The bob reaches a maximum speed of approximately **$0.82 \text{ m/s}$** as it passes through the equilibrium position.
