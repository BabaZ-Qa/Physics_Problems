To solve for the final speed and determine if kinetic energy is conserved, we will use the **Law of Conservation of Momentum** and then compare the kinetic energy before and after the event.

### 1. Identify the Given Values
* **Mass of the runner ($m_1$):** $70 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of the runner ($v_{1i}$):** $3 \text{ m/s}$
* **Mass of the cart ($m_2$):** $140 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of the cart ($v_{2i}$):** $0 \text{ m/s}$ (stationary)



---

### 2. Solve for the Final Speed ($v_f$)
Since the runner jumps onto and stays on the cart, they move together as one combined mass. This is a **perfectly inelastic collision**. 

According to the **Conservation of Momentum**:
$$m_1v_{1i} + m_2v_{2i} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Substitute the known values:
$$(70 \cdot 3) + (140 \cdot 0) = (70 + 140)v_f$$
$$210 + 0 = 210v_f$$
$$210 = 210v_f$$

Now, solve for $v_f$:
$$v_f = \frac{210}{210}$$
**$v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}$**

---

### 3. Check for Kinetic Energy Conservation
To determine if kinetic energy is conserved, we calculate the total kinetic energy ($KE$) before and after the collision.

**Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_i$):**
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_{1i}^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_{2i}^2$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2) + 0$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(9)$$
**$KE_i = 315 \text{ J}$**

**Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_f$):**
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(70 + 140)(1^2)$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1)$$
**$KE_f = 105 \text{ J}$**



---

### 4. Conclusion
**Is kinetic energy conserved?**
**No.** The initial kinetic energy ($315 \text{ J}$) is greater than the final kinetic energy ($105 \text{ J}$).

**Explanation:**
In an **inelastic collision**, kinetic energy is never conserved. The "missing" energy ($210 \text{ J}$ in this case) is transformed into other forms of energy, such as **heat**, **sound**, and the internal work required to deform the objects or stabilize the runner on the cart.
