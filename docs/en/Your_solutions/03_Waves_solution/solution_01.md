To find the wavelength of a sound wave, we use the fundamental wave equation:

$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

Where:
*   $v$ is the **speed** of the wave (m/s)
*   $f$ is the **frequency** (Hz)
*   $\lambda$ (lambda) is the **wavelength** (m)

By rearranging the formula to solve for wavelength, we get:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

### Part A: Wavelength in Air
Given the frequency remains constant at **440 Hz** and the speed in air is **343 m/s**:

1.  **Identify the values:** $v = 343 \text{ m/s}$, $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$.
2.  **Apply the formula:**
    $$\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}$$
3.  **Calculate:**
    $$\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \text{ m}$$

---

### Part B: Wavelength in Water
The frequency of a wave is determined by its source, so it remains **440 Hz** even when moving into a different medium. The speed in water is **1482 m/s**:

1.  **Identify the values:** $v = 1482 \text{ m/s}$, $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$.
2.  **Apply the formula:**
    $$\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}$$
3.  **Calculate:**
    $$\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}$$

---

### Summary of Results
| Medium | Speed ($v$) | Frequency ($f$) | Wavelength ($\lambda$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Air** | 343 m/s | 440 Hz | **0.78 m** |
| **Water** | 1482 m/s | 440 Hz | **3.37 m** |

**Observation:** Since the speed of sound is much higher in water than in air, the wavelength significantly increases while the frequency stays the same.
