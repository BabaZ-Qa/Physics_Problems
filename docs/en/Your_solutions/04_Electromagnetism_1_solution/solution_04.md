To compare these two fundamental forces, we use **Coulomb’s Law** for the electric force and **Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation** for the gravitational force.

### 1. Constants and Given Values
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
* **Proton charge ($q_p$):** $+1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Electron charge ($q_e$):** $-1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Proton mass ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Electron mass ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

---

### 2. Calculate the Electric Force ($F_e$)
The magnitude of the electric force is:
$$F_e = k \frac{|q_p q_e|}{r^2}$$
$$F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(1.602 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Calculate the Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
The magnitude of the gravitational force is:
$$F_g = G \frac{m_p m_e}{r^2}$$
$$F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.11 \times 10^{-31})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$

---

### 4. Calculate the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
To see how much stronger the electric force is, we divide $F_e$ by $F_g$:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}$$
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$$

---

### Summary of Results
* **Electric Force:** $8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$ (Attractive)
* **Gravitational Force:** $3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$ (Attractive)
* **The Ratio:** Approximately **$2.3 \times 10^{39}$**



**Conclusion:** The electric force is roughly **10,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000** times stronger than the gravitational force. This is why, on an atomic scale, gravity is completely negligible and the structure of atoms is governed entirely by electromagnetism.
