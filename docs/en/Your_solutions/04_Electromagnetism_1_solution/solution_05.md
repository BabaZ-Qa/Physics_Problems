To make a proton levitate, the upward electric force must exactly balance the downward gravitational force.

### 1. Identify the Balancing Forces
For the proton to be in equilibrium (levitating), the net force must be zero:
$$\vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_e - \vec{F}_g = 0$$
$$\vec{F}_e = \vec{F}_g$$

*   **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Given by $F_e = qE$, where $q$ is the charge of the proton and $E$ is the electric field strength.
*   **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Given by $F_g = mg$, where $m$ is the mass of the proton and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.



---

### 2. Set Up the Equation
Substituting the force formulas into our equilibrium condition:
$$qE = mg$$

To find the electric field strength ($E$), we rearrange the formula:
$$E = \frac{mg}{q}$$

---

### 3. Substitute the Given Values
*   Mass ($m$) = $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
*   Gravity ($g$) = $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
*   Charge ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$

**Calculation:**
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$
$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$$

---

### 4. Final Result
The required electric field strength is approximately **$1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$** (or V/m).

**Direction:** Since a proton is positively charged, the electric field must point **upward** to provide an upward force against gravity.

> **Perspective:** This is an incredibly small electric field. For comparison, the Earth's natural fair-weather electric field is about $100$ to $150 \text{ V/m}$, which is millions of times stronger than what is needed to levitate a single proton! This happens because the mass of a proton is so minuscule compared to its charge.
