To find the radius of the electron's path, we must first determine its velocity after acceleration and then apply the principles of centripetal force in a magnetic field.

### 1. Calculate the Velocity of the Electron ($v$)
When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference ($V$), its electrical potential energy is converted into kinetic energy:
$$eV = \frac{1}{2}m_e v^2$$

**Constants:**
*   Charge of an electron ($e$) $\approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Mass of an electron ($m_e$) $\approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
*   Potential difference ($V$) = $5000 \text{ V}$

**Solving for $v$:**
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(5000 \text{ V})}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}}}$$
$$v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### 2. Determine the Radius ($R$)
When the electron enters the magnetic field ($B$) perpendicularly, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force:
$$F_B = F_c \implies evB = \frac{m_e v^2}{R}$$

Rearranging for the radius ($R$):
$$R = \frac{m_e v}{eB}$$

**Given:**
*   Magnetic field ($B$) = $0.1 \text{ T}$
*   Velocity ($v$) $\approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$

**Calculation:**
$$R = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg})(4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s})}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(0.1 \text{ T})}$$
$$R = \frac{3.817 \times 10^{-23}}{1.6 \times 10^{-20}}$$
$$R \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Result
The radius of the circular path is approximately **$2.38 \text{ mm}$** ($0.00238 \text{ m}$).

### Summary of Physics Principles
*   **Energy Conservation:** Used to find the speed from the voltage.
*   **Lorentz Force:** The magnetic field exerts a force perpendicular to the motion, causing circular "cyclotron" motion.
*   **Mass-to-Charge Ratio:** The small mass of the electron leads to a very tight turning radius even at high speeds.
