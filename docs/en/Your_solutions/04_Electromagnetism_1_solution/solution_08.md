To find the magnitude of the force acting on the particle, we use the **Lorentz Force** formula for a moving charge in a magnetic field.

### 1. Identify the Formula
The magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$) on a moving charge is given by:
$$F_B = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

**Where:**
*   $q$ is the charge of the particle (C)
*   $v$ is the velocity of the particle (m/s)
*   $B$ is the magnetic field strength (T)
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector

---

### 2. Identify the Given Values
*   **Charge ($q$):** $2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   **Mass ($m$):** $4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ (Note: Mass is not needed for force, only for acceleration)
*   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5\text{ T}$
*   **Speed ($v$):** $10^6\text{ m/s}$
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (Because the particle enters **perpendicular** to the field)

Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, the formula simplifies to $F_B = qvB$.

---

### 3. Calculate the Magnitude of the Force

Substitute the values into the equation:
$$F_B = (2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (10^6\text{ m/s}) \times (0.5\text{ T})$$

First, multiply the charge and the magnetic field:
$$(2 \times 10^{-19}) \times (0.5) = 1 \times 10^{-19}$$

Then, multiply by the velocity:
$$F_B = (1 \times 10^{-19}) \times (10^6)$$
$$F_B = 1 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$$

---

### Final Result
The magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle is **$1 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$**.

> **Direction Note:** While the question only asks for magnitude, the direction of this force would be perpendicular to both the velocity and the magnetic field, determined by the **Right-Hand Rule**. This force will cause the particle to move in a circular path.
