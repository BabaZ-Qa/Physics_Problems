To find the magnetic force vector and its magnitude, we use the cross product version of the Lorentz force law.

### 1. Identify the Formula
The magnetic force $\vec{F}$ acting on a charge $q$ moving with velocity $\vec{v}$ through a magnetic field $\vec{B}$ is:
$$\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

**Given Values:**
*   Charge of a proton ($q$): $\approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($\vec{v}$): $(2\hat{i} - 4\hat{j} + \hat{k}) \text{ m/s}$
*   Magnetic Field ($\vec{B}$): $(\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - \hat{k}) \text{ T}$

---

### 2. Calculate the Cross Product $(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$
We set up the determinant to find the cross product:
$$\vec{v} \times \vec{B} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & -4 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 \end{vmatrix}$$

**Expanding the determinant:**
*   **$\hat{i}$ component:** $(-4)(-1) - (1)(2) = 4 - 2 = \mathbf{2}$
*   **$\hat{j}$ component:** $-[ (2)(-1) - (1)(1) ] = -[ -2 - 1 ] = \mathbf{3}$
*   **$\hat{k}$ component:** $(2)(2) - (-4)(1) = 4 + 4 = \mathbf{8}$

So, $(\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) = (2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} + 8\hat{k})$

---

### 3. Calculate the Magnitude of the Cross Product
Before applying the charge, let's find the magnitude of the resulting vector:
$$|\vec{v} \times \vec{B}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 3^2 + 8^2}$$
$$|\vec{v} \times \vec{B}| = \sqrt{4 + 9 + 64}$$
$$|\vec{v} \times \vec{B}| = \sqrt{77} \approx 8.775 \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### 4. Calculate the Magnitude of the Force ($F$)
Now, multiply by the charge of the proton:
$$F = q |\vec{v} \times \vec{B}|$$
$$F = (1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times 8.775$$
$$F \approx 1.404 \times 10^{-18} \text{ N}$$

---

### Final Result
*   **Force Vector:** $\vec{F} \approx (3.2\hat{i} + 4.8\hat{j} + 12.8\hat{k}) \times 10^{-19} \text{ N}$
*   **Magnitude:** **$1.404 \times 10^{-18} \text{ N}$**

The force is extremely small because it acts on a single subatomic particle, but it is enough to significantly alter the proton's trajectory.
